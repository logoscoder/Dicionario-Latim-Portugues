Moreover, separate powers have separate
functions, as Pope Nicholas attests who
says, as we read in dist. 96, c. Cum ad
verum [c.6, col.339], "That mediator
between God and men, the man Jesus
Christ, distinguished between the duties of
each power by his own acts and separate
dignities." But the secular power and the
ecclesiastical power are separate powers.
Therefore they have separate functions. It is
certain, however, that the act of electing the
supreme pontiff belongs to the
ecclesiastical power. Therefore it does not
belong to the secular power and,
consequently, the emperor ought not to
engage in such an act.
