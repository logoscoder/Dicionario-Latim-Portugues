Student Just as the ecclesiastical order is
thrown into confusion if a bishop does not
preserve his jurisdiction, as the sacred
canons attest (the gloss on Extra, De
accusationibus, c. Sicut olim [col.1600] and
11, q. 1, c. Pervenit [c.39, col.637}), so it is
clear that the order of humanity is thrown
into confusion if the rights of each person
who is in command in temporal affairs too,
and especially he who is supreme ruler, are
not preserved. They can not be preserved,
however, unless they are known. Now I
propose, therefore, that we investigate what
are the rights and the power of the Roman
emperors. Yet in order to begin with the
basics I ask first of all whether the power of
the emperor and the power of the pope are
distinct powers, whether one comes from
the other or not.
