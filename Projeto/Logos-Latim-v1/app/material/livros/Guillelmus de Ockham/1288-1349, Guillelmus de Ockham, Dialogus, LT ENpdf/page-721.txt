Master The reply is that it is not by
divine law that heretics have no property
and no secular dignity, but by human law,
and therefore before heretics were
deprived by human laws of their lordship
of temporal goods they had true lordship
of temporal goods. And because,
therefore, in the time of Julian apostates
and heretics were not deprived of
temporal goods, Julian was a true
emperor and lord of temporal goods.
Afterwards, however, heretics were
deprived of lordship of temporal goods by
the human laws of emperors and the
pope. And from that time, therefore, they
have not been true lords of goods of this
kind. And it is about this [later] time that
the sacred canons speak, not however
about the time when Julian lived.
