Master: The opposite opinion to the above
is that although the emperor specifically
does not have by reason of his imperial
dignity the right to elect the supreme pontiff
or other lesser prelates, yet, in so far as he is
Christian, catholic and believing, the right
to elect the supreme pontiff can belong to
him in such a way that his imperial
elevation does not render him incapable of
a power or right of this kind; on the
contrary, to some extent it renders him
more worthy of a power or right of this
kind.
