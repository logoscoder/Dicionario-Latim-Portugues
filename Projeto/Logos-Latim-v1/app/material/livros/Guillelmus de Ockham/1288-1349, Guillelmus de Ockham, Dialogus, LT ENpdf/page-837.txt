Further, he who is the lord of any temporal
things at all can sell them if he wishes to, 1,
q. 1, c. Eos qui [c.21, col.364], where we
read the following, "Every lord sells what
he has if he wants to, either his slave or
anything else that he possesses." But there
are many temporal things that the emperor
can not sell, nor indeed alienate, because
then he could sell and alienate the empire,
and this is not the fact. Therefore the
emperor is not the lord of all temporal
things.

In addition, he who has a particular portion
in a distribution of temporal things is not
the lord of the other portions which are
granted to others. But an emperor who
captures booty in a just war has a particular

prince." Therefore the emperor is not the
