Master The reply is that in certain cases an
ecclesiastical judge can involve himself in
secular cases by instructing, advising and
even ordering and yet in those cases he can
not punish the secular crimes, even with no
secular judge willing and prepared to show
the fullness of justice, and can not even
pronounce a definitive sentence, but
sentence should be pronounced by a secular
judge who is prepared to do justice; and if
one understands the above gloss in this
way, it is in accord with the truth; if,
however, one understands it otherwise, it
contradicts the sacred canons.
