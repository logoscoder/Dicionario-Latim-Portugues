Master [1] If laymen are subject to both an
ecclesiastical judge and a secular one, even if
for different cases, it can happen that each of
those judges wants to call those laymen to
different places at the same time for a case
which pertains to him or for some common
business pertaining to him and to the laymen
which has to be investigated. They obey,
therefore, either the ecclesiastic or the

neither, and then such a community will not
be best ordered, because that community is
not suitably regulated in which subjects either
